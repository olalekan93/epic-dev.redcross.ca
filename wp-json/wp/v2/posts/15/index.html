European Commission (EC) President Ursula von der Leyen informed on Friday (March 8).<\/p>\n

The BBC reported that Ursula von der Leyen announced in Cyprus that the maritime corridor would allow more aid to be sent to Gaza, which is in a humanitarian crisis.<\/p>\n

The announcement by the head of the European Commission came less than a day after the US announced the construction of a temporary port on the Gaza coast to send humanitarian aid.<\/p>\n

Ursula von der Leyen says,<\/p>\n

Earlier on Thursday, US President Joe Biden made an official announcement about sending aid to Gaza in his State of the Union address. He said that the US military will build a port on the coast of Gaza to send relief to Gaza by sea.<\/p>\n

After the president’s announcement, US officials said that the relief aid sent to the Palestinians through that port will increase significantly and it will be possible to send these relief items in several hundred trucks every day.<\/p>\n

However, the construction of the temporary port will take several weeks to complete, officials said. Large ships can come to this port with food, water, medicines and temporary shelter materials.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
